Hi, I'm Garrett Bess

After nearly a decade competing at the high school, collegiate, and international levels, I turned my passion for volleyball into a coaching career built on one principle: develop the whole player, not just the skill.

Playing experience

My competitive career began at Brownsburg High School (2016–2019) before continuing at Indiana University, where I competed with the IU Club Volleyball program and served as a practice player for the IU Women's Volleyball team (2019–2023). I then took my game international, spending the 2023–2024 season competing for Orkelljunga Volleyball Club in Sweden — an experience that sharpened my understanding of high-level systems and exposed me to coaching philosophies from across the world.

Coaching experience

Over the past five years, I've coached at camps, clinics, and club programs across the globe — working with every age group from elementary-age beginners picking up a ball for the first time to competitive collegiate-level athletes refining advanced skills. I've had the privilege of learning alongside some of the best coaches in the game, absorbing a wide range of technical approaches and systems that I bring directly into every private session.

My approach

Every athlete I work with starts from the same place: the fundamentals. Great volleyball isn't built on highlight plays — it's built on a platform that's consistently in the right position, a serve that holds up under pressure, and footwork patterns that become automatic under stress. We don't skip steps. We build the foundation correctly, then add layers on top of it.

Technical training alone isn't enough, though. I've worked with enough athletes to know that the mental side of the game is just as important as the physical. We work on staying composed through mistakes, building confidence through purposeful repetition, and developing the kind of presence that shows up when it matters most — tryouts, tournament play, and clutch moments.

Private lessons give us something team practice rarely affords: dedicated time. Time to slow down and fix the small things, time to drill the specific skill holding your athlete back, and time to ask questions without the pace of a team environment. Every session is tailored to where your athlete is right now and where they want to be. Whether we're preparing a freshman for her first high school tryout or helping a club athlete push for a college roster spot, the approach is the same — show up, work smart, and build something that lasts.
